My Favorite City: Seattle

My favorite city is Seattle, Washington. I'm very much a homebody and don't travel often, but whenever I do, I find myself longing for the comfort, culture, and familiarity of my hometown. Seattle is the largest city in Washington, home to about 750,000 people. It’s surrounded by water, mountains, and evergreen forests, giving it a unique mix of city life and outdoor adventure. The weather is famous for being unpredictable with long months of dreary gray rainy days and the occasional surprise sunshine. I do absolutely love the rain.

Outdoor Recreation

Seattle's natural beauty is shown throughout its numerous parks and recreational areas. One of my favorite parts about Seattle is how gorgeous it is outside. Whether you're looking for a peaceful nature retreat or a lively waterfront experience, these parks offer something for everyone.

My Favorite Parks in Seattle

  • Alki Beach: Located in West Seattle, Alki Beach Park is a 135.9-acre park featuring a long sandy beach that stretches from Duwamish Head to Alki Point. It's a popular destination year-round, offering amenities such as a jogging and biking path, fire pits, picnic tables, and a bathhouse that serves as an art studio. The park is also historically significant as the landing site of the Denny Party in 1851.
  • Seward Park: This park is located on a peninsula on Lake Washington. Seward Park encompasses 300 acres of forested land. Visitors can enjoy old-growth forest trails, a 2.4-mile bike and walking path along the shoreline, swimming beaches, and beautiful views of the surrounding area. The park also features an amphitheater, an art studio, and a native plant garden.
  • Volunteer Park: Located in tin the Capitol Hill neighborhood, Volunteer Park encompasses 48.3 acres of beautiful maintained gardens and lawns. The park is home to the Volunteer Park Conservatory, the Seattle Asian Art Museum, and a historic water tower that offers a 360-degree view of the city from its observation deck. Visitors can also enjoy walking paths, a wading pool, and open green spaces ideal for picnics and leisure activities. Volunteer Park is also a great place to have a picnic with friends and lay out on the grass, basking in nature.

Woodland Park Zoo

Established in 1899, Woodland Park Zoo has been a cornerstone of wildlife conservation and education in Seattle. Spanning 92 acres, the zoo is home to over 1,000 animals representing nearly 300 species from around the globe. Visitors can explore diverse exhibits such as the African Savanna, Tropical Rainforest, and Northern Trail, each designed to replicate the natural habitats of its inhabitants. The zoo also offers engaging programs, interactive experiences, and beautiful botanical gardens, making it an amazing destination for families and animal enthusiasts alike

To plan your visit, check out the official Woodland Park website .

Historic Music Venues

Seattle's musical heritage is reflected in its historic venues, each offering unique experiences for concert-goers. One of my favorite activities to do is go to concerts, and it is lovely to see how lovely these historic venues are and the deep history behind them. Each of these venues listed offers a unique glimpse into the diverse culture and history of Seattle, ensuring a memorable experience for all visitors.

  • Neptune Theatre: Opened in 1921, the Neptune Theatre in the University District has since transitioned from a movie house to a premier live music venue. With its intimate setting and excellent acoustics, it hosts a variety of performances ranging from bands to comedy shows.
  • The Paramount Theatre: Since 1928, The Paramount has been a cultural landmark in downtown Seattle. Known for its grand architecture and elegant interiors, it showcases Broadway productions, concerts, and film screenings.
  • The Moore Theatre: As Seattle's oldest active theater, The Moore has been hosting a variety of performances since 1907. Its historic charm and diverse programming make it a beloved venue among locals and visitors alike.

Check out upcoming concerts and shows on the STG event calendar.

For more details about Seattle’s historic theaters, visit the official STG Venues website.

Pike Place Market

Established in 1907, Pike Place Market is one of the oldest continuously operating farmers' markets in the United States. It's known for its fresh produce, seafood, and vibrant atmosphere.

Key Attractions at Pike Place Market:

  • Pike Place Fish Market: Renowned for its fishmongers who engage in the famous fish-throwing tradition, delighting crowds with their dynamic displays.
  • Gum Wall: An weird and colorful attraction where visitors have contributed layers of chewing gum, creating a unique and ever-evolving mosaic of thousands of chewed up pieces of gum. Where else would you be able to see something like this?
  • Street Performers: The market's lively atmosphere is enhanced by numerous buskers, offering music, magic, and other performances throughout the day.
  • Delicious food: Pike Place Market is a food lover’s paradise, offering everything from fresh seafood and warm pastries to creamy chowder and artisan cheeses. With so many delicious options, it’s the perfect place to eat your way through Seattle’s iconic market. Explore Pike Place Market's Food Options
